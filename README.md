# The Group Chat: The Gospel According to iMessage 📱✝️

**What if the disciples had a group chat?**

This project reimagines key moments from the Gospels as modern-day iMessage-style group conversations — complete with read receipts, typing indicators, and the kind of unhinged energy that only a group chat can produce.

## The Idea

The people who walked with Jesus were real, complicated, flawed human beings. Peter was impulsive. Thomas asked too many questions. Judas was weirdly fixated on the budget. James and John jockeyed for position. And somehow, through all of it, they witnessed things that changed the course of human history.

This project puts those moments into a format that feels immediately familiar — the group chat — to make the humanity of the disciples accessible to a modern audience. The goal is to make you laugh, make you think, and then make you realize that the theological weight of these events hits differently when you watch real people react to them in real time.

## What This Is

- **Funny.** The disciples roast each other. Thomas asks for KPIs. Peter tries to rebrand as a "water walker." Judas calculates the ROI on miracles. Mary Magdalene drops in at the end of every thread with a perfect summary.
- **Theologically grounded.** Every episode is based on specific scripture passages. The events, dialogue, and theological themes are preserved as faithfully as possible. The humor comes from the *reactions* to real events, not from changing what happened.
- **Humanizing.** The Bible records that the disciples argued about who was the greatest, panicked during storms, and misunderstood parables. This project leans into that because their confusion and growth is what makes their faith relatable.

## What This Is NOT

- A parody or mockery of scripture
- A replacement for reading the Bible
- Theologically liberal reinterpretation — the events and teachings are presented as described in the text

## Running Characters

- **Peter** — Impulsive. Main character energy. Will fight anything including the ocean.
- **Thomas** — Asks questions about everything. Demands KPIs and risk assessments. Occasionally has the best theological take in the room.
- **John** — "The disciple Jesus loved." Will not let you forget it.
- **James** — Big James. Inner circle. Quick with the roast.
- **Matthew** — Former tax collector. Writing everything down. Everything.
- **Andrew** — Peter's brother. The OG recruiter. Often the voice of reason.
- **Judas** — Treasurer. Obsessed with the money bag. Fiscally concerned about miracles.
- **Simon the Zealot** — Anti-establishment. Personally victimized by the Sermon on the Mount.
- **Philip** — Recruited Nathanael. Solid commentator.
- **Nathanael (Bartholomew)** — Will never live down the Nazareth comment.
- **Mary Magdalene** — Always arrives at the end of a thread with the perfect summary and a 💅.
- **Thaddaeus** — "The other Judas." Underrated. Quietly serving.

## How It Works

The site is a single-page app that presents each episode as an iMessage-style group chat. Messages appear one at a time with realistic pacing, simulating the experience of watching a group chat blow up in real time.

**Adding new episodes:** Edit `episodes.js` and add a new episode object to the array. The app automatically picks it up.

## Deployment

Hosted via GitHub Pages. Just a static HTML file + data file. No build step, no framework, no server.
